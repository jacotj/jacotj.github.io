<section class="section">
  <h3>Mission Statement</h3>
  <p>
    The Jacot Lab focuses on pediatric regenerative medicine, with expertise in cardiac tissue, biomaterial interaction, and amniotic fluid and induced pluripotent stem cells. We cultivate a joyful, respectful environment that attracts and trains world‑class students and postdocs.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <h3>Research Focus Areas</h3>
  <h4>Laboratory‑Grown Heart Tissue</h4>
  <p>
    Our goal is to isolate stem cells from amniotic fluid before birth and create personalized heart tissue to repair structural defects postnatally, using the baby’s own cells. Approximately 80% of structural heart defects are detected by ultrasound.  [oai_citation:1‡Jacot Lab for Pediat](https://www.jacotlab.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  </p>

  <h4>Mechanics of Heart Development</h4>
  <p>
    We use lab‑grown heart tissue to study proliferation, migration, and force generation in normal versus genetic‑condition hearts (e.g. Down syndrome, Noonan syndrome, hypoplastic left heart syndrome).  [oai_citation:2‡Jacot Lab for Pediat](https://www.jacotlab.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) [oai_citation:3‡ucdengineeringnews.com](https://ucdengineeringnews.com/q-a-with-jeffrey-jacot/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  </p>

  <h4>Biomanufacturing of Heart Tissue</h4>
  <p>
    We develop scaffolds, bioreactors, and controlled drug‑release platforms to scale regenerative therapies toward cost‑effective clinical application.  [oai_citation:4‡Jacot Lab for Pediat](https://www.jacotlab.com/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  </p>
