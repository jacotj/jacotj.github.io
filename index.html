Engineering Tissue for Congenital Heart Defects

Creating biomaterials and stem-cell derived 3D heart tissue for structural repair in newborns.

Lab Values

  1. Lab members look out for safety and well-being and speak up when needed.
  2. Transparency in decisions and publications, with raw data available.
  3. We train the next generation of bioengineers in a supportive, growth-oriented culture.
  4. We embrace inclusion and equal support for all lab members.
  5. We value the public and philanthropic funds that support our research.

Mission Statement

The Jacot Lab focuses on pediatric regenerative medicine, with expertise in cardiac tissue, biomaterial interaction, and amniotic fluid and induced pluripotent stem cells. We cultivate a joyful, respectful environment that attracts and trains world-class students and postdocs.

Research Focus Areas

Laboratory-Grown Heart Tissue

We aim to isolate stem cells from amniotic fluid before birth and create personalized heart tissue to repair structural defects postnatally, using the babyâ€™s own cells.

Mechanics of Heart Development

We study force generation and cell behavior in hearts with genetic conditions like Down syndrome and hypoplastic left heart syndrome using engineered tissues.

Biomanufacturing of Heart Tissue

We design scaffolds, bioreactors, and drug delivery platforms to move regenerative heart therapies toward clinical and scalable use.
